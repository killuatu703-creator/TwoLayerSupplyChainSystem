Partial outsourcing experiment notes - 2026-06-15

Change:
- Added a simple Delta L condition to outsourcing offer selection.
- Tuned the test parameters so that some jobs have positive Delta L and some do not.

Experiment setting:
- S1 jobs: 16
- S2/S3 existing jobs: 8 each
- Resources: 4
- Outsourcing upper limit: removed in practice by setting it to the S1 job count
- Offer calculation: ThreadPoolExecutor
- Runtime: 137.81 seconds

Result:
- Without outsourcing total_loss: 2916145
- FCFS outsourced jobs: 12 / 16
- EDD outsourced jobs: 12 / 16
- FCFS/EDD total_loss: 82620
- FCFS/EDD no-contract jobs: S1J09, S1J10, S1J11, S1J12

Interpretation:
- The fixed 5-job outsourcing limit is no longer needed for this experiment.
- The simple Delta L condition prevents outsourcing jobs whose expected loss reduction is not positive.
- In this test, 12 jobs are outsourced and 4 jobs are rejected by the Delta L condition.
- This is still a simplified version of the paper's loss function because transportation scheduling and opportunity loss are not fully modeled yet.
