Greedy outsourcing experiment notes - 2026-06-22

Change:
- Added a paper-inspired Greedy outsourcing rule.
- Each iteration evaluates all candidate jobs and all outsourcing suppliers.
- The selected contract is the (job, supplier) pair with the largest positive loss reduction Delta L.

Experiment setting:
- S1 jobs: 16
- S2/S3 existing jobs: 8 each
- Resources: 4
- Compared rules: FCFS, EDD, GREEDY
- Runtime command: /usr/bin/time -p python3 main.py
- Runtime:
  - real: 227.05 sec
  - user: 354.68 sec
  - sys: 19.08 sec

Result summary:
- Without outsourcing total_loss: 2916145
- FCFS total_loss: 82620, outsourced jobs: 12
- EDD total_loss: 82620, outsourced jobs: 12
- GREEDY total_loss: 71010, outsourced jobs: 10

Observation:
- GREEDY produced a lower total_loss than FCFS and EDD in this test.
- GREEDY evaluates more candidate combinations, so runtime increased.
- This is still a simplified version of the paper because transportation scheduling, multi-stage negotiation, and full opportunity loss are not fully modeled.
